My Hobbies

Fly Fishing

I started fly fishing at the beginning of last summer, and found that I loved it. In fly fishing, you use small fake flies that are made of feathers and thread that imitate real flies. I started by fishing for bass with the fly rod and have caught a lot of bass and panfish. I have fished for trout a few times here at Messiah but not to much success.

Fly Fishing

Before I started fly fishing, I fished for bass all the time with a spinner rod, and I still do. My grandpa would always take me out on his boat on the Juniata River. I fish pretty close to my house in Shermans Creek. Last summer, I took my little brother fishing and he caught a massive bass for the size of the creek we were in. Pictured above.

Fly Tying

I started tying flies shortly after I started fly fishing, and I find it very rewarding to make my own "bait" for the fish I am trying to catch. The attention to detail and the beauty of the flies once they are finished makes it wholly worth it, though it is time consuming. Above is a picture of some flies I have tied.

Hunting

I started hunting when I turned 13 and fell in love with it. I love spending time in the quiet of the woods and have also bagged two deer. Though I haven't gotten any deer in a few years simply because I haven't been able to hunt as much, I have high hopes for this coming season.

Shooting

This summer, I got into shooting. I bought a firearm and started practicing my marksmanship. I enjoy things that go boom.

Archery

I shoot archery for fun and also to practice for hunting season. I started archery when I was 14 and have been practicing every summer in anticipation for hunting season ever since. Hopefully I can finally get a deer with my bow this year.

Reading

Right now I am reading the Holy Bible, Confessions by St Augustine, The Life of the Virgin Mary, and Spiritual Struggle by St Paisios the Athonite.

Prayer Rope Making

I started making prayer ropes a few weeks ago. In the Orthodox tradition, we use prayer ropes with special knots (made of small interlocking crosses) to pray the Jesus Prayer with. Recently I have started making my own with cordage and beads. It is very time consuming, but very rewarding. Above is a picture of one that I made.
