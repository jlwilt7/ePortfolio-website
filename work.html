The Morality of Killing in War

I wrote a paper titled The Morality of Killing in War last year for a class. The essay explores the morality of killing in war from a Christian perspective, emphasizing Christ's teachings on pacifism and love for enemies. I acknowledge that war has historically been used as a tool of divine judgment, such as in the conquest of Canaan and the Christianization of Rome.

I use the Bible, writings from early church fathers, and early Christian writers to prove my points. My essay also highlights the tension between the Christian call to peace and the necessity of war in a fallen, sinful world. Ultimately, it argues that soldiers and those in authority can serve as instruments of justice, but they must carry out their duties with caution and humility.

Work Redeemed

This essay, titled Work Redeemed, explores the Christian concept of vocation and how work has been redeemed through God despite the Fall. Before the Fall, Adam and Eve's work was a joyful communion with God, but after, it became burdensome and corrupted by sin. Gene Veith Jr.'s book God at Work emphasizes that vocation is a "mask of God," where God works through people's various callings, even in simple or secular jobs.

I explain that Christians are called to see their work as a way to glorify God and serve others, restoring its divine purpose. Ultimately, vocation should be embraced as a blessing, not a curse, uniting people with God through service.
